EGYPT distinguised by many musical instruments

Egyptian musical instruments are deeply rooted in the country's rich cultural heritage, reflecting its ancient history and traditions. Instruments like the oud (a stringed lute), the qanun (a trapezoidal zither), and the rebab (a bowed string instrument) have been used for centuries in both folk and classical music. Percussion instruments such as the tabla (goblet drum) and riq (tambourine) provide rhythmic depth to performances, while the nay (reed flute) and simsimiyya (plucked lyre) bring soulful melodic qualities. These instruments continue to play a vital role in Egypt's musical landscape, blending the old with the new in contemporary music.
